Common Fishing Techniques

1. Casting & Retrieving

Perfect for bass, walleye, and pike. Cast out your lure and reel it back at different speeds to imitate baitfish. Works great with spinners, crankbaits, and soft plastics.

2. Bottom Fishing

Used mainly for catfish. Cast out a baited hook with a weight and let it rest on the bottom. Recommended baits: nightcrawlers, cut shad, chicken liver.

3. Float/Bobber Fishing

Ideal for beginners and kids. Perfect for bluegill, perch, trout, and crappie. Adjust the float depth until you find where the fish are feeding.

4. Jigging

Drop a jig straight down and lift it repeatedly. Great for cold water fishing and vertical fishing from a boat.

Popular Freshwater Species

Largemouth Bass

  • Bait: Soft plastics, crankbaits
  • Where: Near logs, weeds, docks
  • Tip: Early morning is best

Channel Catfish

  • Bait: Cut bait, stink bait, worms
  • Where: Deep holes, river bends
  • Tip: Night fishing works great

Crappie

  • Bait: Minnows, small jigs
  • Where: Brush piles, submerged timber
  • Tip: They school together — if you catch one, stay there
